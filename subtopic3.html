The future of virtual reality: Prospect and problems

Author

Andrew Alfonso Lie

Abstract

Virtual reality (VR) is a technology that allows users to interact with a computer-generated environment using devices like headsets and simulators. It creates new experiences and allows users to explore situations that may not be possible in real life. VR has strong potential in fields such as education, healthcare, and entertainment. However, challenges like motion sickness, high costs, and limited content slow its widespread use. Improvements in visual quality, motion response, and sound are needed to enhance the user experience. If these issues are addressed, VR can grow further and become a valuable tool in many industries.
